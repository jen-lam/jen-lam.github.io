What does this mean?

We are increasing the cognitive load of customers by deviating from familiar patterns

Poor scannability and findability

The More menu is an ever-growing list of entry points with no structure. Stakeholders view this screen as a way to bring more visibility to their features, but that is lost as more links are added

Lack of hierarchy and categorisation

The current hierarchy does not reflect how customers use the menu, or the natural groupings of tasks and information. Customers are spending unnecessary time trying to find information or features that are relevant for their needs

Disjointed omni-channel experience

To access account information, customers are pushed into a different, web view experience. It is not immediately obvious how to return to the app, and customers may end up continuining their shopping experience in a buggy web view

Inconsistent with global standards

When looking at competitors, Woolworths lacks key features and personalisation that enrich the customers self-service experience. As customers form expectations from their experiences with other apps, we are making it harder for them to perform simple tasks

"I have to scan every one of these until I find something that resonates."

We have the opportunity to

Increase More Account menu interactions

By strengthening the brand of More -> Account, creating a place for all things personal and account-related

Increase engagement of self-service features

By improving findability through increasing visibility and bringing meaningful structure to all self-service features

Reduce overall Customer Hub costs

By making features and information more readily available, and bringing prominence to asynchronous support channels

Improve app adoption, VOC and NPS

By making it easier to access information and manage personal details and security natively
