My Account

Re-imagining how customers manage their personal details, orders, payment methods, and access value on the Woolworths app.

Role: Product strategy, Research, Ideation, Interface Design
Duration: Jan 2025 - March 2025

Overview

Background

Problems

A deep dive blah blah say how the problems were discovered

Poor scannability and findability

The More menu is an ever-growing list of entry points with no structure, especially when stakeholders want to bring more visibility to their features

Lack of hierarchy and categorisation

The current hierarchy does not reflect how customers use the menu, or the natural groupings of tasks and information

What does this mean?

We are not supporting customer top tasks, not enabling personalisation, and missing out on acquisition opportunities

Disjointed omni-channel experience

Accessing account information pushes customers into a different, web view experience where a lot of content is available on app.

Inconsistent with global standards

Customers form expectations from their experiences with other apps

What does this mean?

We are increasing the cognitive load of customers by deviating from familiar patterns

"I have to scan every one of these until I find something that resonates.

We have the opportunity to

Increase More Account menu interactions

By strengthening the brand of More -> Account, creating a place for all things personal and account-related

Increase engagement of self-service features

By improving findability through increasing visibility and bringing meaningful structure to all self-service features

Reduce overall Customer Hub costs

By making features and information more readily available, and bringing prominence to asynchronous support channels

Improve app adoption, VOC and NPS

By making it easier to access information and manage personal details and security natively
